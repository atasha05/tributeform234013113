Nikola Tesla: The Master of Alternating Current

Tesla's Revolutionary Timeline

  • 1856: Born in Smiljan, Austrian Empire (modern-day Croatia).
  • 1882: Conceives the idea for the **rotating magnetic field**, the principle behind nearly all AC machinery.
  • 1888: Invents the **induction motor** and patents the system for generating, transmitting, and using alternating current (AC).
  • 1891: Invents the **Tesla coil**, an electrical resonant transformer circuit used to produce high-voltage, high-frequency AC.
  • 1893: Showcases the power of AC by illuminating the **World's Columbian Exposition** in Chicago.
  • 1895: The Niagara Falls hydroelectric power plant, using Tesla's AC system, begins operation, delivering long-distance power.
  • 1943: Dies in New York City, leaving behind a legacy of over 300 patents worldwide.
