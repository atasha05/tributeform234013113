Nikola Tesla: The Master of Alternating Current

Tesla's Revolutionary Timeline

  • 1856: Born in Smiljan, Austrian Empire (modern-day Croatia).
  • 1882: Conceives the idea for the rotating magnetic field, the principle behind nearly all AC machinery.
  • 1884: Arrives in the United States and briefly works for Thomas Edison.
  • 1888: Invents the **induction motor** and patents the system for generating, transmitting, and using alternating current. This invention ignites the "War of Currents" against Edison's direct current (DC).
  • 1891: Invents the **Tesla coil**, an electrical resonant transformer circuit used to produce high-voltage, high-frequency alternating current.
  • 1893: Showcases the power of AC by illuminating the World's Columbian Exposition in Chicago with George Westinghouse, a spectacular display known as the "City of Light."
  • 1895: The Niagara Falls hydroelectric power plant, using Tesla's AC system, begins operation, delivering long-distance power to Buffalo, New York.
  • 1899: Moves to Colorado Springs to conduct high-voltage, high-frequency experiments, including early work on wireless transmission.
  • 1943: Dies in New York City, leaving behind a legacy of over 300 patents worldwide.
